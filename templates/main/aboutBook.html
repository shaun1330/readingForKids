The KEY ingredients for effective early reading instruction are step-by-step instructions in:
  • Phonemic or sound awareness
  • Knowledge of letter-sound relationships known as phonics
  • Word building
  • Vocabulary development
  • Reading accessible texts where words are easy to sound out and are part of a young child's vocabulary

The program begins with phonemic or sound awareness activities.

These are oral activities with nine different activities, including:

  • Sound discrimination
  • Using children's stories to play with speech sounds
  • Rhyming Game
  • Sound Boxes
  • Counting Sounds in Words

You and your child will enjoy these clearly explained, important pre-reading activities.

A lesson plan shows you how to teach your child to recognise letters.

You are shown how to teach your child to write upper and lower case letters.

The step-by-step program begins next
